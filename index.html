Massimo Ostuzzi

Since October 2023, I am a Ph.D. student at Ruhr University Bochum, under the supervision of Alex May and Michael Walter. My research is about {classical, quantum} public-key cryptanalysis, with a special focus on understanding isogeny-based constructions.

I’m part of the EU project Marie Curie Doctoral Network QSI (Quantum Safe Internet) together with other eleven PhDs working all over Europe.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics in 2021 from University of Padova, under the supervision of Matteo Longo. In 2023, I was awarded with a Master's degree in Mathematics from University of Padova, under the supervision of Alessio Caminata and Alberto Tonolo.
